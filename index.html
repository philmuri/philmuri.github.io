Project on Anomalies in Quantum Mechanics google-drive

Mathematica  •  QFT  •  Quantum Physics  •  Research

Summer 2021

Institution: University of Southern Denmark (SDU)

Supervisors: Thomas A. Ryttov

This paper - tied to an individual studies project - explored anomalies in systems where classical symmetries are broken at the quantum level, particularly focusing on their manifestation in quantum field theory and simpler quantum mechanical systems. It delves into the scale invariance of mechanical systems like the inverse-square and Dirac delta function potentials in two dimensions, analyzing the effects of quantization, regularization, renormalization, and anomalous symmetry breaking.

First-Year Project on Decay Properties of the Higgs Boson

Mathematica  •  Particle Physics  •  Theoretical Physics  •  Modelling & Simulation

Winter 2017

Institution: University of Southern Denmark (SDU)

Supervisors: Heidi Rzehak & Mads Toudal Frandsen

A throwback to my first academic project! The project investigated the decay properties of the discovered 125 GeV Higgs boson, focusing on its decay through the bottom - anti-bottom quark channel. We computed the decay rate within the framework of the Standard Model, analyzing theoretical aspects, relativistic kinematics, and quantum corrections to achieve realistic results. Despite initial deviations from theoretical expectations, incorporating quantum corrections and higher-order terms enhances the accuracy of the computed decay rate.

About Me

My Passions

It is often difficult for me to summarize my interests, as I constantly develop new ones and go back to explore older ones. Nevertheless, some activities have stuck with me since childhood and are there to stay! I come from a very musical family, and I am passionate about music. I have played the piano since I was ten, with a particular focus on classical pieces and a particular adoration of Chopin's pieces. I have played many smaller concerts, both solo and duets with cello.

Besides music, I have throughout my studies developed a strong passion for programming, with a particular focus on data science. My passion has only grown with the recent advancements in the field of big data. Much of my freetime these days is spent learning programming languages and tools, new frameworks and all things machine learning - while also following the newest trends and developments herein. Lately I also developed a curiosity for both web-based and app-based game development; some of my recent projects can be found under my portfolio section!

Interpersonal Skills

I am a passionate guy who constantly seeks challenges and exciting new tasks within all areas of programming - as well as fundamental research in astrophysics, particle physics and cosmology. I strive to create valuable and beautiful results with the skills that I possess, so that I can be proud to present and hopefully inspire others to develop a genuine interest in the topics I work with. While I am very quality-conscious, I am by no means strict with enforcing my visions; I value a comfortable work environment where ideas can be shared to enhance the end results with many perspectives.
